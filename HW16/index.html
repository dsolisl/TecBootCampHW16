States that have adopted Medicaid Expansion have significantly improved health coverage

There is no clear correlation between poverty levels and health coverage. There is a clear correlation between states that have adopted Medicaid expansion and health coverage.

Texas is an outlier state, with the highest (24%) of population lacking health coverage, significantly higher than states with a higher percentage of their populations under the poverty line. Texas has not expanded Medicaid or Medicaid funded family planning.

States that have only implemented Medicaid funded family planning but not Medicaid Expansion per se have not seen increased health coverage overall.
